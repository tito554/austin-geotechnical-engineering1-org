Contaminated Soil Remediation in Austin – Field Testing & Cleanup Support

Rigorous testing. Clear reporting.

LEARN MORE

Austin sits on the Balcones Fault Zone, a geological transition that creates complex soil conditions. About 12% of the city’s land has some degree of historical contamination from industrial sites or old fuel storage. We handle contaminated soil remediation in Austin from start to finish. Our team collects samples at depth using direct-push rigs and sends them to an accredited lab under EPA Method 8260 for VOCs and 8270 for SVOCs. For projects requiring immediate field data, we combine this work with a permeability test at the site to understand how fast contaminants might migrate through the clay-rich soils typical of the Blackland Prairie. We also check groundwater flow direction using monitoring wells. Every step follows ASTM D1586 for sampling consistency and ASTM D2487 for soil classification.

Austin’s clay-rich soils require careful sampling protocol — one wrong depth can misrepresent the entire plume.

Process overview

Austin’s hot summers and flash-flood patterns mean remediation windows can be tight. The city’s underlying marl and chalk layers (from the Austin Chalk formation) hold contaminants differently than the alluvial soils along the Colorado River. We adjust our approach accordingly. For shallow contamination, we often recommend excavation with off-site disposal. For deeper plumes, we use in-situ chemical oxidation or bioremediation. Before choosing a method, we run a full characterization including grain-size distribution, pH, and organic content. A key step is correlating contamination levels with soil strength data from a direct shear test to see if the soil can support excavation slopes without caving. We also evaluate nearby structures using georadar surveys to locate buried tanks or pipes before digging. The lab holds ISO 17025 accreditation for the relevant EPA methods, and we follow ASTM D422 for particle size.

Local context

A redevelopment project near Lady Bird Lake hit a problem. Old dry-cleaning solvents were found 12 ft below grade, right above the water table. The owner initially wanted to skip remediation and just cap the area. But the plume was moving toward the lake. We did a detailed investigation and found concentrations of PCE at 85 µg/L, well above Texas’s Residential Cleanup Level of 5 µg/L. Ignoring that would have led to state fines and long-term liability. We designed a dual-phase extraction system that removed 97% of the mass in six months. The lesson: contaminated soil remediation in Austin is not optional when redeveloping near waterways.

FAQ

How much does contaminated soil remediation cost in Austin?

Typical projects range from US$3,380 to US$13,750 depending on contamination depth, volume, and treatment method. Excavation with disposal is usually on the lower end; in-situ chemical oxidation or vapor extraction runs higher. We provide a firm quote after initial sampling.

What regulations apply to soil cleanup in Austin?

The Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ) oversees remediation under the Texas Risk Reduction Program (TRRP). Federal EPA methods apply for analysis. Local ordinances also require notification before excavation near the Edwards Aquifer recharge zone.

How long does remediation take for a typical residential lot?

For a quarter-acre lot with shallow petroleum contamination, excavation and disposal usually take 5 to 10 working days. In-situ treatment can run 4 to 8 weeks depending on injection rounds and post-treatment sampling. We always provide a schedule estimate before starting.
